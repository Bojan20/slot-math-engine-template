## 1. Parties

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of
`{{effective_date}}` (the "Effective Date") by and between:

- **"Operator"** — `{{operator_name}}`, a `{{operator_entity_type}}`
  organized under the laws of `{{operator_jurisdiction}}`, with principal
  offices at `{{operator_address}}` ("Operator"); and
- **"Vendor"** — `{{vendor_legal_entity}}`, a `{{vendor_entity_type}}`,
  developer and licensor of the Slot Math Engine platform, with principal
  offices at `{{vendor_address}}` ("Vendor").

Operator and Vendor are each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties."

## 2. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship involving
Vendor's slot mathematics engine, including the closed-form solver
portfolio, the intermediate representation ("IR") specification, the
certification dossier toolchain, and related material (the "Engine"),
and have determined that the exchange of confidential information is
required for this evaluation (the "Purpose").

## 3. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by
one Party (the "Discloser") to the other (the "Recipient"), whether in
written, oral, electronic, visual, or other form, that is either marked
"Confidential" (or with a similar legend) or that a reasonable person
would understand to be confidential given its nature and the
circumstances of disclosure, including without limitation:

- **Vendor confidential:** closed-form solver kernels (source and binary
  form); IR specification revisions not yet published; Monte Carlo
  validation harness internals; pricing, discount schedules, and term
  sheets; pipeline of partner discussions; unreleased game templates;
  certification dossier templates and lab response packets.
- **Operator confidential:** mathematical specifications for unreleased
  game titles; RTP and volatility targets; player behavior data; wallet
  provider integration details; jurisdictional certification roadmaps;
  internal financial forecasts; partner and supplier lists.
- **Mutual:** the existence and content of these discussions, including
  the fact that the Parties are in negotiation.

## 4. Permitted Use

Recipient may use Confidential Information **solely for the Purpose**
and for no other commercial purpose. Recipient shall:

1. Limit access to its employees, contractors, and professional advisors
   who have a need to know and who are bound by confidentiality
   obligations at least as protective as this Agreement.
2. Protect the Confidential Information with the same degree of care it
   uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance,
   and in no case with less than reasonable care.
3. Not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any software or
   binary artifacts disclosed under this Agreement, except to the
   extent expressly permitted by law that cannot be waived by contract.
4. Not use Confidential Information to develop, market, or sell
   products or services that compete with the Discloser's offerings.

## 5. Carve-Outs

Confidential Information does **not** include information that:

- (a) is or becomes generally available to the public other than as a
  result of a breach by Recipient;
- (b) was rightfully known to Recipient on a non-confidential basis
  prior to disclosure, as evidenced by Recipient's written records;
- (c) is rightfully received from a third party not under a duty of
  confidentiality to Discloser;
- (d) is independently developed by Recipient without use of or
  reference to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by
  Recipient's written records.

## 6. Required Disclosure

If Recipient is required by law, regulation, court order, or regulatory
authority (including a gaming control board) to disclose Confidential
Information, Recipient shall, where lawfully permitted, give Discloser
prompt written notice and reasonable cooperation in seeking a
protective order or other limitation on disclosure. Recipient shall
disclose only the minimum portion of Confidential Information legally
required.

## 7. Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an
initial term of **three (3) years**, unless earlier terminated upon
thirty (30) days' written notice by either Party. Notwithstanding
termination, each Party's obligations of confidentiality with respect
to Confidential Information disclosed during the term shall survive for
an additional **two (2) years** following termination (the "Survival
Tail"), and indefinitely with respect to trade secrets that remain
protected under applicable law.

## 8. Return or Destruction of Materials

Within thirty (30) days after the earlier of (a) the conclusion of the
Purpose; (b) Discloser's written request; or (c) termination of this
Agreement, Recipient shall, at Discloser's election:

1. Return all Confidential Information, including all copies, extracts,
   and derivatives, to Discloser; or
2. Destroy all Confidential Information and certify destruction in
   writing.

Recipient may retain one (1) archival copy in its secure legal files
solely for compliance purposes, and shall not be required to delete
Confidential Information from automated electronic backups that are
overwritten in the ordinary course, provided that such retained copies
remain subject to the obligations of this Agreement.

## 9. No License; No Obligation

Nothing in this Agreement grants Recipient any license, right, title,
or interest in or to any Confidential Information, intellectual
property, or other rights of Discloser, except the limited right to
use the Confidential Information for the Purpose. Neither Party is
obligated by this Agreement to enter into any further commercial
agreement or transaction.

## 10. No Warranty

ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED **"AS IS"**, WITHOUT WARRANTY
OF ANY KIND, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION
WARRANTIES OF ACCURACY, COMPLETENESS, MERCHANTABILITY, FITNESS FOR A
PARTICULAR PURPOSE, OR NON-INFRINGEMENT.

## 11. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that monetary damages may be inadequate for a
breach of this Agreement and that the non-breaching Party may seek
injunctive or other equitable relief, in addition to all other remedies
available at law or in equity, without the necessity of posting bond.

## 12. Governing Law and Venue

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with
the laws of `{{governing_law}}`, without regard to its conflict-of-laws
principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the
state and federal courts located in `{{governing_law_venue}}` for any
dispute arising out of or related to this Agreement, except that
either Party may seek injunctive relief in any court of competent
jurisdiction.

## 13. Assignment

Neither Party may assign this Agreement, in whole or in part, without
the prior written consent of the other Party, except that either Party
may assign this Agreement without consent to a successor in interest in
connection with a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of
substantially all of its assets, provided that the assignee assumes all
obligations hereunder in writing.

## 14. Severability

If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or
unenforceable, that provision shall be modified to the minimum extent
necessary to render it valid and enforceable, and the remaining
provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

## 15. Entire Agreement; Amendments

This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties
regarding its subject matter and supersedes all prior or
contemporaneous understandings. Any amendment must be in writing and
signed by authorized representatives of both Parties.

## 16. Counterparts; Electronic Signature

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts (including by electronic
signature platforms such as DocuSign or Adobe Sign), each of which
shall be deemed an original and which together shall constitute one
and the same instrument.
