## Why this exists (the problem)

Every commercial math engine in the market today couples a game's math
to its engine binary. To certify a new title you re-run the whole
TestU01 + NIST + PractRand + cross-platform parity pipeline against that
engine version, paying lab fees of $40–80k per game and waiting 6–10
weeks for a UKGC/MGA sign-off. To change a paytable you re-certify. To
swap jurisdictions you re-certify. To verify a player dispute 5 years
later you find the right binary, the right OS, and pray it still runs.

This engine inverts that. The math is the IR. The engine is the IR
**interpreter**, certified once, hosting an unbounded library of IR
fixtures. RTP is **closed-form** when the topology allows; MC is **bit-
identical** on the same seed across TS, Rust, Linux, macOS, and
(scheduled) Windows. Every cert artefact is regenerated by `npm run`
from the same IR + the same engine commit hash — for any audit, today
or in 5 years.
