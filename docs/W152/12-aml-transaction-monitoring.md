The 2025 revisions to Rec 1 (risk-based approach) do not relax Rec 22’s core expectation that casinos apply CDD, record-keeping, and SAR obligations at cash/transaction thresholds, but they reinforce risk-proportionality (FATF, 2025).- **FinCEN Oct 2025 SAR FAQs:** Clarified that transactions at/near the $10k CTR threshold alone do not mandate a SAR absent structuring intent; also stated continuing-activity SARs should cover 90-day periods after the initial SAR, but created confusion by suggesting 30-day filing from “initial detection” rather than the prior 90-day update rhythm—this appears to conflict with legacy guidance and further clarification is expected (Brownstein, Oct 2025).- **EU 6AMLD & AMLR:** 6AMLD adopted 19 June 2024, giving Member States 3 years to transpose; expands criminal liability for aiding/abetting and 22 predicate offenses. The parallel AMLR introduces a single rulebook, caps cash at €10,000, and establishes AMLA in Frankfurt (mid-2025). Gambling operators are explicitly in scope, with no exemption for online slots (G2RS, 2025; TwoBirds, 2025).- **UK MLR 2017 updates:** Jan 2024 amendments updated PEP treatment and high-risk third-country lists. July 2025 POCA changes raised the defense-against-money-laundering threshold from £1,000 to £3,000. HM Treasury’s July 2025 National Risk Assessment upgraded casino ML risk from “low” to “medium.” The UKGC updated casino AML guidance in Oct 2025 to demand B2B relationship risk assessments (Chambers UK, 2025).- **MGA AML/CFT:** Malta applies the €2,000 CDD threshold on a rolling 180-day basis (stricter than 4AMLD’s single-transaction floor). The MGA and FIAU conducted 43 AML/CFT examinations in 2024; six licensees faced remediation totaling ~€185,000. In H1 2025, 11 exams were initiated and three remediation directives issued (MGA Annual Report 2024; MGA Interim Report 2025).- **Germany GwG:** The AML Act (implementing 5AMLD, amended Jan 2021) requires online gambling operators—excluding gaming halls/VLTs—to perform annual risk analyses and ensure payment-account holder identity matches the player account. Anonymous payment methods are prohibited. BaFin updated its Interpretation and Application Guidance in 2025 (Chambers Germany, 2025; Sumsub, 2025).- **Italy 231/2007:** Applies to all online gaming with cash winnings. ADM issued new AML Guidelines on 18 Nov 2024 requiring implementation within 30 days. CDD triggers at €2,000 for bets or prizes. STRs must be filed via ADM’s online AML reporting system. Record retention is 10 years. Supply-chain partners (B2B platforms, outsourcing providers) can be drawn into AML controls (Chambers Italy, 2025).- **Trigger Thresholds:** Industry consensus identifies four high-priority slot-specific flags: (1) cumulative session losses disproportionate to declared income/affordability; (2) rapid deposit-withdrawal cycles with minimal play (the classic “wash”); (3) dormant-account reactivation followed by large deposits or immediate withdrawals; and (4) bonus abuse used as a laundering proxy—where players exploit wagering requirements to justify high-volume, low-risk play to clean funds (SEON, 2025; GLI-19; Sumsub, 2025).- **Real-Time vs Batch:** FATF and the UK FCA recognize both, but the market is shifting to hybrid architectures: real-time for sanctions/PEP hits and velocity spikes; batch (T+1 or slower) for behavioral clustering and structuring detection. The EU AMLR’s emphasis on “timely” reporting is pushing more operators toward real-time transaction monitoring (Lucinity, 2024; SEON, 2025).- **Slot Engine vs Operator Telemetry Boundary:** The slot engine/game server must emit per-spin metadata (timestamp, bet amount, win amount, game ID, round seed/session ID, RTP band) to the operator’s platform in real time or near-real time. AML scoring—velocity rules, win-cluster anomaly detection, risk rating—stays operator-side. GLI-19 explicitly places “monitoring player accounts for deposits and withdrawals without associated game play” on the *operator*, not the game supplier (GLI-19; Spinlab, 2025).- **PEP/Sanctions Feeds:** Operators integrate Refinitiv World-Check (human-vetted, deep historical data, batch-heavy), Dow Jones Risk & Compliance (high data integrity), or ComplyAdvantage (API-first, real-time, <500ms screening) at onboarding and for ongoing monitoring. The UKGC’s Oct 2025 PEP guidance updates require continuous screening, favoring API-native vendors (ComplyAdvantage, 2026; ChainScore, 2026).- **Recent Enforcement:** UKGC fined Platinum Gaming £10m (Oct 2025), ProgressPlay £1m (Aug 2025), and Videoslots £650k (Nov 2025) for AML/SR failures.
