**TL;DR:** Modern slot math (2024–2026) has shifted RTP density from base game to bonus features, with high-volatility titles parking 55–65% of RTP in features. Hold & Win uses geometric-reset respin chains tuned by coin-value distributions and collector-symbol probability. Bonus-buy pricing increasingly follows volatility-multiplier tiers rather than pure EV, often with divergent RTP. Max-win caps truncate the payout tail and interact with jurisdictional stake limits (UK £2/£5, Germany €1) to clip achievable multiplier ceilings.---### Key Findings- **Hold & Win recalibration** — The feature is a Markov reset process: 3 respins, each new bonus symbol resets the counter. The probability of filling a 5×3 grid is driven by per-cell hit probability *p*; collectors gather all visible coin values, acting as high-variance RTP amplifiers within the feature. (TimeOfSpin, GoldenPiñata)- **Mystery symbol math** — xWays Mystery Chests reveal 1–4 identical symbols, expanding ways from 729 to 11,664 (Pixies vs Pirates). In cluster games, mystery symbols transform post-spin into a single random pay symbol, redistributing probability mass across the paytable rather than adding new paylines. (KeyToCasinos, BigWinBoard)- **Bonus-buy pricing models** — Two schools compete: (1) EV-based pricing (cost ≈ average bonus win / target RTP) and (2) volatility-multiplier tiers (Hacksaw/Nolimit City entry points at 50x–500x stake) where higher prices promise elevated symbol densities or persistent modifiers. (WizardOfVegas, GamingAmerica)- **Free-spins retrigger curves** — Expected length follows the geometric series *E* = *n₁p₁* / (1 − *n₂p₂*). Real implementations truncate this at a hard cap, but studios rarely disclose the truncation point, creating a material gap between theoretical and realized spin counts. (SlotDesigner)- **Multiplier-accumulator mechanics** — Gates of Olympus 1000 uses additive orb stacking (50x + 50x = 100x) with no cap per tumble; Sugar Rush 2 uses grid-position multipliers capped at x128. Product-based multipliers are rare because they produce runaway variance that breaches test-lab stability thresholds. (FreeSlots99, SugarRush2)- **Cluster-pays variance amplification** — On a 7×7 grid, 8+ symbol clusters scale non-linearly in payout while remaining exponentially rare. High-volatility cluster titles typically allocate only 35–45% of RTP to the base game and 55–65% to free spins, where persistent multipliers compound across cascades. (SugarRush2, SlotDecoded)- **Jackpot carve-out impact** — Progressive jackpots divert 1–4% of each stake to the pool. Mega Moolah discloses 88.12% base RTP + 8.80% jackpot contribution = 96.67% total. GLI-19 mandates that the variable contribution of such awards to displayed RTP must be clearly disclosed. (Online-Casinos, GLI-19)- **Max-win cap clipping** — A cap truncates the right tail of the distribution and piles probability mass at the ceiling. UK operators commonly enforce £250,000 per-wager payout limits; UKGC stake limits (£2 under-25, £5 over-25) further constrain the max achievable multiplier at regulated stakes. GLI-19 requires the highest advertised award to hit at least once per 100M games unless actual odds are shown. (Slingo, Sky Terms, GLI-19, Gov.uk)---### Contradictions / Open Questions- **Bonus-buy RTP parity:** Sweet Rush Bonanza claims its buy feature maintains the *same* RTP as base play, whereas Gates of Olympus 1000 certifies 96.50% base vs 96.23% bonus buy. The industry has not converged on whether buy features should be RTP-neutral or carry a small volatility premium. (SweetRushBonanza vs FreeSlots99)- **Geometric vs truncated geometric:** The textbook model assumes infinite retrigger depth, but every real slot caps retriggers. The expected-spin delta between pure geometric and hard-capped models can exceed 20%, yet truncation parameters are almost never published.
