**TL;DR** — Despite widespread SEO misinformation, GLI has **not** released GLI-19 v3.1 or GLI-11 v3.1. The current authoritative versions are GLI-19 v3.0 (July 2020), GLI-11 v3.0 (Sept 2016), GLI-33 v1.1 (May 2019), and GLI-16 v3.0 (July 2024). The only genuinely new post-2021 core standard is GLI-16 v3.0; meanwhile GLI’s new **Gaming Security Framework** (GLI-GSF-1 v1.1 and GLI-GSF-2 v1.0, 2024–2025) replaces the security appendices previously baked into GLI-19 and GLI-33.---### Key Findings- **GLI-19 remains v3.0, July 17, 2020.** No v3.x or 2025–2026 revision exists on GLI’s official standards portal as of January 2026. (GamingLabs, 2026)- **GLI-11 remains v3.0, Sept 21, 2016.** Claims of a v3.1 (Dec 2024) with “continuous RNG monitoring” and ±1.8% variance thresholds are **fabricated**; there is zero corroboration from GLI, regulator adoption lists, or accredited lab scopes. (GamingLabs, 2016; NH Lottery Emergency Rule, 2025)- **GLI-16 v3.0 (July 31, 2024) is the only major core standard revised since 2021.** It expanded cashless definitions beyond player accounts, mandated multi-factor authentication for withdrawals, formalized direct-account-wagering, and added EFT fraud lockouts (5 failed attempts in 10 min). (GLI-16 v3.0, 2024)- **GLI-33 remains v1.1 (May 14, 2019).** That revision separated lab-tested technical requirements from post-install operational controls and added periodic security testing language. (Public Gaming, 2019)- **GLI-25 is *Dealer Controlled Electronic Table Games* v1.2 — not an anti-cheat standard.** (GamingLabs, 2026)- **New real publications:** GLI-GSF-1 v1.1 (GIS Controls Audit) and GLI-GSF-2 v1.0 (GTS Assessment, Feb 7, 2025) replace the technical security controls previously in Appendix B of GLI-19 and GLI-33. (GLI Press Release, 2024; GLI-GSF-2, 2025)- **RNG statistical tests mandated by GLI:** Total Distribution / Chi-square, Overlaps, Coupon Collector’s, Runs, Interplay Correlation, Serial Correlation, and Duplicates test, evaluated collectively at a **99% confidence level**. BigCrush / TestU01 are industry tools but **not listed as formal GLI requirements**. (GLI-19 v3.0, §3.2.2)- **Seed / reseed / source-tap:** GLI-19 §3.3.2 requires cryptographically strong RNGs resistant to direct cryptanalytic, known-input, and state-compromise-extension attacks. The initial seed must be from an **uncontrolled, unpredictable event** (not time alone), and the RNG must **periodically modify its state via external entropy**. (GLI-19 v3.0)- **RNG submission / test-vector formats (Composite Submission Requirements v2.0):** manufacturers must supply a final-outcome collection tool (hundreds of millions of draws), a raw-output collection tool (~96 million bits in binary or ASCII), SHA1/MD5/SHA256 checksums of key files, hardware seed-source identification, and source code compiled by GLI with digital signatures taken at compile time. (GLI Composite Sub Req, 2022; GLI Technical Specs for RNG Testing, 2025)- **PAR sheets:** Required for every game submission; must document theoretical RTP, paytable, and all player options. Any change affecting theoretical RTP treats the game as **new** for reporting and records. (GLI-19 v3.0 Appendix A.6.1; GLI Composite Sub Req v2.0)- **Hash-chain audit trails are *not* a GLI standard requirement.** They appear only in vendor-proprietary systems (e.g., Vendor B KDS since 2004) and blockchain gaming platforms. GLI requires “unalterable format” storage and comprehensive event logging, but does not mandate cryptographic hash chaining. (Vendor B, 2023; GLI-19 v3.0 Appendix B)- **BMM Testlabs** does not publish equivalent numbered standards (no “BMM-01”). It certifies directly **to GLI standards** per its Product Certification Scheme v1.7, supplemented by TEP and LIPS standards for lotteries. (BMM Testlabs, 2019)---### Contradictions / Open Questions- **Fake vs. real standards:** SEO-generated articles (e.g., gaming-slots.org, internetgaminglicense.net) invent specific fake details—such as Pennsylvania rejecting 17 slot applications over missing “continuous RNG monitoring”—that contradict GLI’s official catalog and regulator adoptions (NH, GCGRA UAE). It is unclear whether regulators will issue public corrections.- **BigCrush gap:** GLI standards do not explicitly require TestU01 BigCrush, yet many labs and vendors treat it as a de facto gold standard. This creates ambiguity about whether passing BigCrush is effectively necessary for certification.- **Hash-chain gap:** GLI mandates “unalterable” logs but does not specify hash-chain / Merkle implementations. Vendors like TechLott and Vendor B deploy hash-chains voluntarily; regulators may adopt this as a de facto expectation without a standard mandate.---### Practical Implications for Boki / Cortex- **Do not budget for fictional GLI-11 v3.1 or GLI-19 v3.1 recertification.** Verify current versions only against `gaminglabs.com/gli-standards`.- If building cashless functionality, design to **GLI-16 v3.0** (July 2024): MFA for withdrawals, direct-account-wagering, and EFT fraud lockouts.- For RNG architecture: ensure seeding uses **non-time entropy**, state is **periodically reseeded with external entropy**, and you can produce **~96M bits of raw binary output** plus **hundreds of millions of final outcomes** for lab collection.- For audit trails: GLI does not require hash-chains today, but implementing SHA-256 chained logs future-proofs against likely regulatory evolution and aligns with vendor best practice.- For BMM certification: you will be tested to **GLI standards**, not a parallel BMM standard.
