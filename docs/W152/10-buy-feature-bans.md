**TL;DR** — Bonus-buy slots are banned in the UK, Netherlands, and Germany, and Denmark is legislating a ban. They remain permitted in Italy, Spain, Romania, Ontario, and US states (NJ, PA, MI). Sweden is contested: public pressure favors prohibition, yet licensed studios still ship the feature. The industry has largely moved to jurisdiction-gated config flags, though some regulators still require separate certified binaries.---### Key Findings- **UK — Banned.** The UKGC does not name “bonus buy” in primary legislation, but RTS requirement 14A states products must not “actively encourage customers to … increase their stake.” A January 2020 enforcement warning explicitly targeted “feature buy-in” facilities, and the October 2021 slots reform package cemented the interpretation. Studios now ship UK builds with the feature deactivated. (UK Gambling Commission, 2020; London Post, 2026)- **Netherlands — Banned.** The KSA’s *Remote Gambling System Assessment Scheme* classifies “bonus buys” (alongside “buy spins” and “buy feature”) as impermissible forms of autoplay/staking in casino games against the licensee, because they allow consecutive games without affirmative player action between each game. (Kansspelautoriteit, System Assessment Scheme v2.1)- **Germany — Banned.** GlüStV 2021 explicitly prohibits the “Feature Bonus Kauf.” The GGL also enforces a €1 maximum stake per spin and a mandatory 5-second spin delay, which makes bonus buys mechanically impossible in the regulated market. (Coincierge.de, 2026; GGL)- **Denmark — Ban incoming.** A broad coalition agreement of 24 October 2025 (*Gambling Package 1*) includes “a ban on purchasing direct access to bonus games on slot machines.” Bills are proceeding through the Folketing. (Lexology / Bird & Bird, 2025)- **Sweden — Permitted, politically contested.** Play’n GO’s February 2024 polling showed 55% of regular Swedish slot players want bonus buys prohibited, but Spelinspektionen has not issued a ban. Sweden-licensed providers (e.g., Nolimit City, Octoplay) continue to certify and offer feature-buy slots. This creates a direct contradiction with affiliate sites that claim the feature is “banned under Swedish license.” (Play’n GO, 2024; CasinoWow)- **Italy, Spain, Romania — Permitted.** No regulator has explicitly prohibited in-game bonus purchases. Romania’s licensed market actively lists Bonus Buy as a popular slot category. (Nightrush; Chambers Gaming Law 2025 — Italy/Spain/Romania)- **Belgium — Ambiguous.** Promotional bonuses, gifts, and free games are banned under the September 2024 amendments, but the Gaming Commission clarified that in-game mechanisms “inherent to participation in the game and form part of the game’s rules” are not prohibited. Bonus buys have not been formally classified either way. (Two Birds, 2024; Chambers Belgium, 2025)- **Ontario / US (NJ, PA, MI) — Permitted.** Ontario’s AGCO restricts advertising of inducements and bans autoplay, but does not prohibit bonus-buy mechanics. US state regulators (DGE, PGCB, MGCB) focus on transparency of terms and geolocation, not on the feature itself. (AGCO; GamixLabs)- **Technical compliance — Config flags are standard, but with caveats.** Industry best practice is a modular build where bonus buy is toggled server-side per jurisdiction. However, testing labs note that “some markets require separate approvals for each game version,” and the UKGC’s testing strategy historically required that disabled features be demonstrably unreachable. (GamixLabs, 2026; Source Code Lab, 2026)---### Contradictions / Open Questions- **Sweden’s true status:** Is bonus buy banned or not? Primary regulatory sources are silent, while commercial providers certify the feature for Sweden. The claim of a ban appears to be a misreading of Sweden’s strict *promotional* bonus caps.- **Single binary vs. separate build:** Vendors market “one codebase, many markets,” yet labs and some regulators treat a materially different feature set (bonus buy present vs. absent) as a distinct product requiring re-certification.
