**TL;DR** — Despite heavy crypto-industry hype, zero-knowledge (ZK) proofs are not yet used for per-spin slot RNG in any licensed or major unlicensed casino. The real “provably fair” state-of-art remains commit-reveal SHA-256/HMAC (Stake.com, BC.Game, Bitcasino) or ECVRF (Rollbit). Rollbit upgraded to an elliptic-curve VRF (ECVRF) using the RFC 9381 `SECP256K1_SHA256_TAI` cipher suite, allowing players to verify outcomes with a public key and proof without ever revealing the private key (Rollbit, 2025; RFC 9381, 2023).- **ZK circuits (Halo2, Plonky2, Risc0, SP1) are absent from slot RNG production.** These systems dominate zkML and blockchain scaling, but no 2024-2026 production slot platform uses them for per-spin randomness. Switchboard provides TEE-secured verifiable randomness with 400 ms standard latency (2–5 ms on its “Surge” tier) (Pyth Network, 2026; Switchboard, 2025). Neither claims GLI-19 certification.- **Threshold/distributed RNG exists but is niche for gambling.** Drand (League of Entropy) emits threshold-BLS randomness every 3 s.
