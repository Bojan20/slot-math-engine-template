# Warm CMO — Pre-meeting Email After Intro

> Summary line 1: Pre-meeting email to Vendor B CMO after warm intro from mutual contact.
> Summary line 2: Tone: market-narrative, ship-cadence framed, NOT technical.
> Summary line 3: Goal: confirm meeting, share one-pager, set non-engineering expectations.

## Tone notes
- CMOs respond best to a short, well-framed market story + one visual.
- Pre-read = one-pager, NOT deep-dive (which is too dense for marketing leadership).
- Thank the intro contact publicly in the email; reinforces social bond.
- ≤140 words.

## Subject line variants
- A: "Re: intro from {{intro_name}} — one-pager pre-read for {{day}}"
- B: "Looking forward to {{day}} — quick pre-read on cadence acceleration"
- C: "Thanks for the intro — one-pager + ROI snapshot for our call"

## Body
Hi {{first_name}},

Thanks to {{intro_name}} for connecting us — really helpful.

Quick framing for {{day}} at {{time}}: my one-page outreach summary is here — {{one_pager_link}}. It captures the market-narrative side in 4 quadrants: cadence numbers, differentiators, lab matrix, pilot path. ~3 minutes to read.

For the call, I'll come prepared with:
- The ROI model (5-year NPV scenarios: conservative / base / aggressive).
- The competitive frame vs Pragmatic / Vendor D / Hacksaw / Push Gaming current cadence.
- Three real customer-narrative storyboards (30-sec elevator, 5-min deep, 90-min board) you could borrow internally.

What's the most useful angle to lead with from your side? Always better to calibrate before the call than to guess.

Best,
{{sender_name}}
{{sender_phone}} | {{sender_link}}
